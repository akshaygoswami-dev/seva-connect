Road Safety: Every Journey Matters

4 min read • Updated: June 2026

India loses over 1.7 lakh lives to road traffic accidents every year — roughly one death every three minutes. Behind every statistic is a family shattered, a community diminished. Road safety is not just a matter of traffic regulation; it is a fundamental public health crisis that demands awareness, behavioural change, and collective accountability.

The Reality on Our Roads

Two-wheeler accidents account for nearly 44% of all road fatalities in India, and the single most effective prevention measure — wearing a helmet — is ignored by a staggering majority of riders. In Silchar and across the Barak Valley, narrow roads, poorly lit stretches on NH-6, waterlogged surfaces during monsoon season, and the widespread absence of footpaths make every journey perilous for riders, drivers, and pedestrians alike.

Drunk driving remains a leading killer, responsible for roughly 4.5% of recorded fatal crashes nationally — a figure widely believed to be underreported. Seatbelt usage in rear seats is virtually non-existent, despite evidence that rear-seat belts reduce fatality risk by up to 25%. In the Barak Valley, overloaded auto-rickshaws and speeding on the Silchar-Lakhipur and Silchar-Hailakandi corridors contribute to frequent and avoidable tragedies.

Key Facts

  • India records the highest number of road accident deaths globally — over 1,73,000 in 2023 alone.
  • Wearing a helmet reduces the risk of fatal head injury by 42% and serious head injury by 69%.
  • Pedestrians and cyclists account for nearly 20% of all road traffic deaths in the country.
  • Assam ranks among the top 10 states in India for road accident fatalities per lakh population.

What You Can Do

  • Always wear a helmet: Whether you are the rider or the pillion passenger, a certified ISI-marked helmet is your single most important line of defence. No journey is too short to skip it.
  • Buckle up — every seat: Seatbelts must be worn by every occupant, not just the driver. Rear-seat passengers are equally at risk during sudden collisions.
  • Never drive under the influence: Alcohol impairs reaction time, judgement, and coordination. If you drink, hand the keys to a sober companion or hire a ride. Zero tolerance saves lives.
  • Respect pedestrian spaces: If you are on foot, use designated crossings and walk facing traffic on roads without footpaths. If you are driving, slow down near schools, hospitals, and market areas.
  • Report hazards: Potholes, missing signage, and broken streetlights can be reported to local municipal authorities. Proactive reporting prevents accidents before they happen.
